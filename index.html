VLSI 2026 · University of Toronto / Rice University

A Split-Inference Intracortical Interface IC for Battery-Free mm-Scale Brain Implants.

Mustafa Kanchwala*, Jianxiong Xu*, Mohammad Abdolrazzaghi*, Wonjune Kim, Gerard O'Leary, Yu Huang, Junyu Ma, Jose Sales Filho, Sudip Nag, Hanfeng Cai, Qiaosong Deng, Weian Deng, Anush Mutyala, Theeban Kumaresan, Shidu Ren, Chae Lim, Mandana Movahed, Homeira Moradi, George Eleftheriades, Taufik A. Valiante, Jacob T. Robinson, Roman Genov

We present a split-inference intracortical interface IC for battery-free, millimeter-scale brain implants, with validation across motor reach-to-grasp and inner-speech decoding. The work connects low-power neural decoding, spike-sorting pipelines, and system-level evaluation, showing neural output data-rate reductions of 1,066× and 16,732× while preserving decoding performance.
